  • Moving a link into a lower, less populated category is the simplest way to insure it will continue being a free link
  • Adding a link to a subcategory can get it into a more descriptive and relevant category. Upper categories are more general while subcategories get more and more specific

Step 2 Add Regional Filters (optional)
  • Adding regional information about your link can get it displayed more prominently. The number of links in that region and category is less than the Global level so you avoid the competition and are more likely to be on the first page
  • Adding regional information about certain types of local oriented businesses such as hairdressers, barbers, coffee shops, tire shops etc ends up saving everyones's time and resources with lower cost for the advertisers. Users in California, for example, do not care about seeing a link to a barber shop in Washington, D.C. for example.
  • Regional Filters is a natural for cell phone users. Cell phone users are often away from their homes and offices and, thus, do not have access to the "Yellow Pages". Adding Regional Information is a great way to reach that ever growing segment of the market, especially if you are a local business..
